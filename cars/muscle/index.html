History of Muscle Cars

Muscle cars are high-performance automobiles, typically American, built for straight-line speed and power. They rose to prominence in the 1960s and 1970s, becoming iconic symbols of American automotive culture.

The classic muscle car era produced legends like the Ford Mustang, Chevrolet Camaro, and Dodge Charger. These cars were known for their powerful V8 engines, aggressive styling, and affordable price compared to European sports cars.

Famous Muscle Car Models

1969 Chevrolet Camaro

The Camaro SS combined sleek design with raw power, making it a legend on and off the track.

1970 Dodge Charger

With its distinctive grille and powerful V8 engine, the Charger became an icon of muscle car culture.

1965 Ford Mustang

The Mustang started the pony car craze, blending style, performance, and affordability.
